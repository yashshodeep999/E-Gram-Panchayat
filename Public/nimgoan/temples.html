History & Cultural Significance

Spiritual Heritage

The name Nimgaon Ketaki comes from the Ketaki bushes that once grew abundantly here. While working in the fields, villagers discovered a Shivalinga which gave the village spiritual identity.

The Yatra of Shri Ketakeshwar Maharaj is celebrated with devotion on Chaitri Ekadashi with a grand chariot procession. The Matang community traditionally pulls the chariot and the Chikhle family maintains the Shivlinga.

Suvarnayug Trust organizes the Ganesh Yatra on 25th and 26th December, adding to the village’s festive atmosphere.

For visitors: Please keep the temple premises clean, follow queue discipline and respect local traditions.

Shri Ketakeshwar Mahadev Mandir (श्री केतकेश्वर महादेव मंदिर)

This sacred temple houses the ancient Shivalinga discovered in the Ketaki bushes. It is the heart of the village’s spiritual heritage.

Suvarnayug Ganesh Mandir (सुवर्णयुग गणेश मंदिर)

Managed by the Suvarnayug Trust, this temple is dedicated to Lord Ganesha and is the center of the December Ganesh Yatra celebrations.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

When is Shri Ketakeshwar Maharaj Yatra?

It is celebrated on Chaitri Ekadashi with a grand Rath (chariot) procession.

When is Suvarnayug Ganesh Yatra?

It is organized on 25th and 26th December every year.

What are the basic temple rules for visitors?

Maintain cleanliness, avoid loud sounds, respect traditions and follow queue discipline.
